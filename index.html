Background

The Foundation in Lucknow

My perspective as a creator was born in Lucknow, a city that provided the backdrop for my earliest encounters with technology. Born on April 27, 2010, I spent my early childhood at The Tree House, where the first seeds of curiosity were planted. This environment fostered a natural inclination toward understanding the mechanics of the world around me, setting the stage for a life defined by technical and artistic inquiry.

The years between 2014 and 2021 at St. Fidelis College were essential in shaping my academic discipline. It was here that I began to move beyond simple curiosity and started exploring the digital landscape as a serious medium. This period was marked by a steady transition from being a consumer of digital content to becoming a student of its architecture, learning the importance of structure, logic, and precision in everything I built.

Currently, I am navigating the intersection of engineering and design at Sunbeam School in Robertsganj. This current chapter is focused on the rigorous application of physics and mathematics to creative problems. By blending school-level engineering principles with professional-grade digital tools, I am working toward a future where technical complexity and aesthetic beauty are inseparable.

The Stack

C# and Systems Logic

My approach to programming is rooted in the architecture of C#. I view code as the skeletal system of any digital experience, requiring both flexibility and strength. I specialize in building modular engine components and systemic gameplay modules that can scale without friction. This logical foundation extends to Python and Lua, allowing me to bridge the gap between pure data and interactive systems.

Advanced 3D in Blender

In the realm of Blender, my work focuses on the philosophy of hard surface modeling. I prioritize clean topology and procedural workflows, ensuring that every asset is optimized for real-time rendering without sacrificing visual fidelity. I enjoy the challenge of translating complex mechanical designs into digital geometry that feels weighted, functional, and grounded in physical reality.

Visual Direction and Cinematography

I apply professional cinematography techniques to every frame I render. By understanding the physics of light and the psychology of camera movement, I can direct a viewer's attention and evoke specific emotional responses. This involves a deep study of narrative lighting, focal lengths, and composition, ensuring that the visual story is as compelling as the technical execution.

Anomalies

The Experimental Log

Beyond the structured world of design pipelines and game architecture, I have always been driven by a "break it to understand it" mentality. This often manifests in hardware projects that push my environment to its limits. One of my most persistent challenges was turning an aging laptop into a functioning Hackintosh. This process involved a deep dive into kernel patches and driver kexts, fighting against hardware limitations to force an operating system into a space it was never meant to occupy. It was a lesson in persistence and the intricate layers of system-level software.

Not every experiment has been a quiet technical success. There was a notable instance where my curiosity regarding electrical load led to short circuiting my entire room. While it was a momentarily startling event, it served as a very practical introduction to the fundamental laws of electricity and the critical importance of safety protocols in hardware engineering. It remains a reminder that innovation often requires pushing past the safe boundaries, even if it occasionally ends in darkness.

I have also spent time building custom hardware, such as assembling a functional MP3 player from scratch. Projects like these allow me to appreciate the tactile side of technology, stepping away from the screen to work with physical circuit boards and tactile controls. These anomalies in my workflow are not just hobbies, they are the sandbox where I test my problem-solving skills without the guardrails of professional software, ensuring that I stay connected to the raw, fundamental mechanics of technology.
